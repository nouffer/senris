The Centre for Environmental Justice (CEJ) is an environmental conservation and public interest litigation organization in Sri Lanka with key objectives to:
  • mprove environmental justice, good governance, and ecological democracy
  • Promote sustainable development and environmentally responsible communities,
  • Encourage community participation in decision-making on natural resources.
  • Safeguard nature, ecosystem and defend frond frontline communities from human rights violations and campaigns
  • Promote Environmental Justice and equity through legal and other means.

As the natural environment is at risk today, CEJ believes that environmental protection should be done at all levels while the government has a major role to play, every citizen has a responsibility to care for the environment in which they live. It is in this endeavor the CEJ planned out a new initiative to protect the environment, to ensure equal environmental rights for all people, and to promote ecological sustainability by supporting environmentally sound community activities by the project on “Safeguarding Environment and Natural Resources and working towards sustainable development through awareness and advocacy”.

Mannar and Ampara Districts have taken as the project area for intervention under three main components:
  1. dentification of Other State Forests (OSF) in Mannar and Ampara Districts and updating the locational data with field observations to provide evidence-based information to halt the cancellation of the 5/2001 circular.
  2. Conducting a Biodiversity Study within the identified OSF and ecological significance of the forests and wildlife areas in the districts to advocate the issues threatening their existence and sustainabilit
  3. Land Use Analysis to understand the implications of releasing the lands for different uses as proposed by the Government and to leverage the advocacy programs to convince relevant authorities and decision-makers in lobbying the sensitive issues with the law and decision-makers for advocating corrective interventions.

Crowdsource incidents of forest destructions using WebGIS and Mobile App developed to report to the “Safeguarding Environment and Natural Resources Information System” to demarcate and update the incident-based spatial maps regularly.
